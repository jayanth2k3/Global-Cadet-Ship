B.Sc. Nautical Science – Complete Career Guidance

B.Sc. Nautical Science Programme

Approved & Industry-Oriented Maritime Education

Approved by the Directorate General of Shipping (DGS), Mumbai, Government of India

Offered under Technical Collaboration with Alagappa University, Tamil Nadu

3-Year Full-Time Residential Programme

The B.Sc. Nautical Science Programme is designed for students aspiring to build a successful career as Deck Officers in the Merchant Navy and progress toward becoming a Captain/Master Mariner of a ship.

This comprehensive programme provides students with strong theoretical knowledge, practical maritime training, leadership development, navigation skills, cargo operations knowledge, and onboard safety management in accordance with international maritime standards.

Upon successful completion of the pre-sea training programme, cadets become eligible to join merchant vessels as Deck Cadets / Trainee Nautical Officers. Leading national and international shipping companies regularly conduct campus recruitment drives, and guidance for placement opportunities is provided to deserving and meritorious students.

Training / Campus Photo

Career Progression & Opportunities

The programme is structured across six semesters over a duration of three years, leading to the award of a B.Sc. Nautical Science Degree.

After completion of the course, cadets join ships as Trainee Deck Cadets and undergo mandatory onboard sea training. Upon successful completion of the required sea service and certification process, candidates become eligible to obtain the Certificate of Competency as a Second Officer.

With additional sea experience, professional examinations, and operational expertise, officers can steadily progress through the ranks of Chief Officer and eventually achieve the prestigious position of Captain / Master Mariner.

A career in Nautical Science offers global employment opportunities, excellent salary packages, leadership roles, and the opportunity to work in the international maritime industry.
